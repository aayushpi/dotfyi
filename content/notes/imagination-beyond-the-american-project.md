A fascinating speculation of what it means to shatter the lens of nationalism and look at communities without borders. The reading list at the very end is some great speculative fiction recommendation.

> I wonder if our attachment to the structure and container of America constrains our imaginations from envisioning what could actually bring those ideals to life. Humans organize to take care of each other and the ecosystem of which we are part and on which we depend. Society is born of mutual need.

>The United States’ sovereignty is demonstrated by armed guards at an arbitrary border. The specific mythology of this country’s “freedom” is built on the illusion of agency imparted by independence, self-reliance, and hard work (why’s it always gotta be hard?).

> We occupy a geography that’s been designated as America. We are impacted by it, but no one lives in America. I live on my block, among my neighbors and the people I see at the grocery store and the gym.

The project of Nationalism is of the enforcement of limits of empathy: to put rails that make us look inward for both the mundane and the muse, to change the name of the feeling of comfort from solidarity to security, to constrict us where we should be growing.
